Addiction

Neurobiological syndrome composed of impulsive and compulsive behaviors (Koob & Volkow, 2010)
and characterized by:

  1. Compulsive search and consumption of the substance
  2. Lose of control to limit consumption
  3. Emergence of a negative emotional and motivational state

Treatment

The optimal treatment is a multidisciplinary approach involving psychotherapy, counseling and pharmacology.

Not effective enough:
Abstinence rates:
40-60% (McLellan et al., 2000)

It is necessary to search for new treatments

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation

Allows to modulate cortical excitability by the usage of focalized electromagnetic pulses that can pass painlessly through the scalp and skull
(Noohi & Amirsalari, 2016)

rTMS in addiction

Recent research support the use of rTMS over the prefrontal cortex to reduce tobacco, alcohol and cocaine craving (Barr et al., 2011)

  1. The dorsolateral prefrontal cortex is an important part of the meso-fronto-limbic dopaminergic system (Barr et al., 2011)
  2. rTMS may induce dopamine liberation in cortical and subcortical areas (Cho & Strafella, 2009)
  3. The prefrontal cortex is inolved in decision-making processes, which might be altered in addicted patients (Barr et al., 2011)

Magnetic Resonance Imaging

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is widely used in addiction research because of its safety, absence of radioactivity and flexibility of the obtained information (Suckling & Nestor, 2017)


Functional MRI

Functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) is a modality of MRI where brain activity can be indirectly measured by the endogenous contrast of the oxygen level in the blood (BOLD signal)
(Ogawa et al., 1993)

Functional connectivity

Neurophysiological activity relatively synchronized in distinct brain regions
(Friston, 2011)

These connectivity data can be studied as networks composed of brain regions connected by functional associations; they share characteristics with other biological networks and systems, and, because of this, they can be categorized using methods of complex networks.

Network Science

Interdisciplinary effort to characterize the properties of complex systems by the quantification of the topologies of their respective network representations.
(Rubinov & Sporns, 2010)

These analysis can reliably quantificate brain networks with a small number of metrics easily computable and neurobiologically significative.
Bullmore, 2007; Humphries & Gurney, 2008; Latora & Marchiori, 2001)

de Fornito, Bullmore y Zalesky, 2017

de Farahani, Karwowski y Lighthall, 2019

Wang et al., 2015

Are there clinical and network topology changes in cocaine-addicted patients after a treatment with repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation?

This was a mono-centric, double-blinded, placebo controlled randomized clinical study with parallel groups, carried out completely in the National Institute of Psychiatry "Ramón de la Fuente Muñiz" in Mexico City.

This study was part of a major project financed by CONACYT (S0008-2015-2-2610971, and approved by the Ethics committe of the Institute (CEI/C/070/2016).

Sample

We recruited treatment-seeking cocaine-addicted voluntaries from both the addiction clinic at the Institute and other clinics and institutes around Mexico City that met the DSM-5 criteria for cocaine-dependence.

A subsample of matched healthy controls was taken from the database of a previous project of the Institute (Garza-Villarreal et al., 2017).

rTMS

  • Magpro R-30 MagVenture;
    MCF-P-B70 coil (8-shaped)
  • DLPFC: 5cm anterior; 2cm lateral to M1
  • Two 5 Hz sessions;
    30 min interval
  • 50 10s trains;
    1 min inter-tren interval; 100% MT
  • 5000 pulses:
    2500 pulses, 58 min/session
  • Double-blinded Sham;
    frontal muscle electrode

Network construction

Undirected weighted networks were constructed from the correlation (Pearson's $r$) of the BOLD signal from each of the 264 nodes of the Power atlas.

Conclusions

This is the first double-blind study with a sample of more than 40 participants and neuroimaging measures of the effects of rTMS in cocaine addiction.

Our results defend the usability of rTMS over the left DLPFC as a feasible alternative in the treatment of cocaine dependence, specially in those patients in which conventional treatment is not sufficient

This work lays the groundwork to continue the exploration of rTMS as an addiction treatment, and the usage of network science methods in the study of the neurobiological bases of addiction, its development and evolution.
